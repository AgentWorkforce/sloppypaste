Definition

Sloppypaste (noun / verb)

Copy-pasting raw LLM output into a conversation without reading it, editing it, or summarizing the relevant part.

The problem is not using AI. The problem is quietly handing the work of finding the signal to the person on the other side.

Why it's frustrating

Sloppypastes are rarely malicious. They are just exhausting.

  • They arrive as a wall of confident, generic filler.
  • The real point is often hiding somewhere in paragraph six.
  • No one says which sentence actually matters.
  • The recipient ends up doing the editing job.

The simple rule

Before you paste AI output:

  1. Read it.
  2. Delete most of it.
  3. Highlight the useful part.
  4. Explain why you're sharing it.

If you skip those steps, you might be sloppypasting.

Friendly closing note

AI is fine. Sharing ideas is fine. Editing still matters.

Use AI all you want. Ask it for drafts, lists, options, code, and weird little thought starters. Just do not make other people read the whole output for you.

The polite move is simple: take ninety seconds, keep the good part, and send that instead.
